Antireligion; why atheism is insufficient.

Anything that requires the indoctrination of children and suppression of rational thought is evil. Therefore, I'm against religion as a general rule, but not necessarily against religious people.

“Isn't that racist?”

No. Religion isn't a race.

Also, racism is equally stupid. No one chooses their birthplace, parents, or species, so being proud on something you're not responsible for is folly. It follows that pride in general inherently makes no sense because most of what happens can be attributed to luck ().

“Isn't that Islamophobic?”

Yes. And in a sense that word is a dysphemism; that is, it's trying to make the thing sound worse than it is. Whereas in fact there's nothing wrong with being scared of profoundly dangerous ideas or the purveyors thereof. Similarly, people against war—pacifists—generally aren't slandered as “warphobists”, except for the occasional “hippy”.

Why resort to dysphemisms? People feel personally attacked when their ideas get scrutinized because there lies great difficulty in separating one's ideas from one's identity. They take it personally. This isn't just a problem with religious folk, it applies to the general populace. Instead of self-reflection—questioning the validity one's thoughts—it's easier to silence opponents with slander. Dirty tactic, but unfortunately it works.

Why atheism is insufficient.

The problem already starts at the definition of atheism: “the doctrine or belief that there is no God.” . Even though it is equally silly to believe in gods as it is to believe in the Flying Spaghetti Monster, why bother, for we can't prove or disprove either*, and we can't spy on or control people's thoughts anyway. As long as people don't act on irrational convictions there is little harm in believing there are supreme beings.

Atheism too sparingly addresses the crux of its importance: it's not the individual belief in gods that does harm—though gullibility is a catalyst—instead it's the (collective) religious actions that may flow from it†. As with many bad things in society, these actions are characterized by “smart” people taking advantage of the gullible, hence why religion starts at birth. There's nothing easier than to mold the mind of a defenseless impressionable child. What begins with a seemingly innocuous form of baptism and communion soon segways into prayer, i.e. scare tactics, which in turn leads to confession, i.e. medieval mass surveillance. Ultimately one begets a cult that practices paying for absolution, celibacy leading to rampant sexual molestation , stoning, female genital mutilation, witch hunting, castigating homosexuality, you name it.
