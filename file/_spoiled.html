The boons and banes of being spoiled.

I've never had to work, which has it's ups and downs. It's only after losing something that you realize its value. In my case, every time a firetruck passes, remorse sets in; I shouldn't have quit.

Preface

At age 31, dealing with the fallout of Covid-19, I've come to realize the up- and downsides of being spoiled. Materially spoiled, because poor was my intellectual or emotional upbringing. Many would call me ignorant, not realizing how lucky I was with the chances I got, which is true to a degree, but not the whole story.

“They couldn't give me a lot of material things, but they gave me morals, courage, and most important of all, love.” —Philip Banks

My parents meant the best, my situation was the opposite.

Quitting.

My biggest flaw and regret: serial quitting.
